Why most candidates apply blind

The gap between qualified and hired

There's a persistent, frustrating gap in hiring: people get rejected for roles they could do well. Not because they aren't capable — but because their resume doesn't speak the language of the specific role, team, or organization they're applying to.

The Gap Finder is designed to close exactly that gap. It compares what a specific job description is looking for against what your background actually contains — and tells you, with specificity, what's missing and how to address it.

The difference between a gap and a disqualifier

Not every gap is a deal-breaker. The Gap Finder distinguishes between high-priority requirements (the ones hiring managers will ask about in an interview) and lower-priority preferences (nice-to-haves that won't sink your application).

Knowing which gaps matter most helps you prioritize where to spend your time. A High-priority gap might be worth addressing before you even apply. A Low-priority gap might just need a single framing tweak in an existing bullet.

What if I'm genuinely missing a requirement?

That's worth knowing before you apply, not after you get rejected. The Gap Finder will flag requirements you genuinely don't have — and the recruiter's take section will give you an honest read on whether the gap is likely to disqualify you or whether your other strengths could compensate.

Sometimes the honest answer is: this isn't the right role to apply to yet. More often, the answer is: you have what they need, you just haven't communicated it correctly. The Gap Finder helps you tell the difference.

Should I use this before or after tailoring my resume?

Use the Gap Finder first. It shows you what's missing before you do any rewriting — so you know exactly what to add and surface when you tailor. Then use the Resume Tailor or Fit Scanner to execute the changes and verify your score improved.

The three tools work best in sequence: Gap Finder (understand the gaps) → Resume Tailor (fix them) → Fit Scanner (verify your score).
