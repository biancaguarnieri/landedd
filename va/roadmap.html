Step-by-step guide

The VA application roadmap.

Federal hiring is a defined process with predictable steps. Here's exactly what happens between finding a job and starting it — and where to use Landedd VA to maximize your chances at each stage.

1
Search
Find the right role on USAJobs

Go to usajobs.gov and filter by Department of Veterans Affairs. Narrow by location, occupation series, or pay grade. VA clinical roles span nursing, medicine, pharmacy, social work, psychology, and dozens more.

  • Search by seriesOccupation SeriesA 4-digit code for every federal occupation. 0610 = Nurse, 0602 = Physician, 0660 = Pharmacist, 0185 = Social Worker. The series determines which hiring rules apply. code (e.g., "0610") to find all nurse postings across every VA facility.
  • Filter by "Open to the public" — VA also posts internal-only roles that you won't qualify for unless you're already federal.
  • Note the closing date. Most VA postings close within 5–10 days of opening.

Answer the questionnaire strategically

Most VA postings include a scored questionnaire — usually 20–30 questions, each tied to competencies in the JOA. Your score determines your ratingRatingA score (typically 70–100) assigned to your application based on questionnaire responses and minimum qualifications. Scores at or above 90–95 are typically "Best Qualified.". Only candidates who score high enough get referred to the hiring manager. The questionnaire is the gate — most applicants don't know how to answer it to maximize their score.

  • Answer honestly, but use the JOA language. If you have the experience, say so in the same words the JOA uses.
  • HR can verify your questionnaire responses against your resume. If they don't match, you're disqualified.

6
Submit
Submit through USAJobs before the deadline

Upload your documents through USAJobs.gov before the posting closes. Deadlines are strict — most close at 11:59 PM Eastern time. Late submissions are not accepted.

  • Upload: federal resume, official transcripts (if required), professional license, and veteran preference documentation if applicable.
  • Make sure your USAJobs profile contact info is current — HR may reach out via email or phone listed in your profile.
  • Check your USAJobs application status page after submitting. "Received" → "Reviewed" → "Referred" or "Not Referred."
7
Rating
Your application gets rated by HR

HR reviews your questionnaire score and verifies minimum qualifications. This is largely automated. You'll receive one of three statuses on USAJobs: Referred (you made the list), Not Referred (you scored too low or didn't meet a requirement), or Ineligible (you didn't meet an SPF).

  • Wait time varies — typically 2–8 weeks depending on the facility's hiring timeline and posting volume.
  • "Not Referred" often means your questionnaire score was below the cutoff, not that you're unqualified for the role.

8
Hiring manager review
Your application goes to the hiring manager

If referred, the hiring manager reviews the referred pool and selects candidates to interview. The pool is typically a "Best Qualified" list — top scorers from the rating step. Being referred doesn't guarantee an interview.

  • You can follow up after 4 weeks without news. Contact the HR point of contact listed in the JOA — not the hiring manager directly.
  • Hiring managers may reach out via phone. Answer unknown numbers during this window.
  • A posting may stay open for multiple selections — being referred keeps you in consideration even after initial interviews.
9
Interview
Interview — often a panel, sometimes includes a skills check

VA clinical interviews often involve a panel (nurse manager, charge nurses, or clinical educators). For bedside roles, you may also complete a skills demonstration or simulation. The process mirrors private-sector interviews but with a federal formality.

  • Prepare behavioral questions using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
  • Know the VA's mission and your facility's patient population. Panel interviewers notice when candidates have done their homework.
  • Ask about the onboarding timeline — VA credentialing takes time, and knowing the expected start date helps you plan.
10
Offer + onboarding
Tentative offer → credentialing → official start date

A tentative offer comes first — it's conditional on background check, drug screen, medical review, and credentialing (for clinical roles). The official offer follows once everything clears. VA credentialing is thorough: license verification, malpractice history, education verification. Plan for 4–10 weeks between tentative offer and your official start date.

  • Respond to all HR document requests promptly — delays on your end extend the timeline.

Quick reference
Key terms at a glance

Every acronym you'll see in the VA application process — explained in plain English.

A Job Opportunity Announcement is the official federal job posting on USAJobs.gov — a legal document that defines exact requirements, pay, and how to apply. Read the entire posting before applying. Missing one requirement = automatic rejection.
Title 38 governs VA clinical hiring (nurses, physicians, pharmacists). No 2-page limit, no essay questions. Title 5 is standard federal hiring — 2-page limit and KSA essays required. Hybrid 38 blends both.
The Nurse Professional Standards Board sets starting pay grades for VA nurses (Series 0610). Your grade (Nurse I, II, III) is based on education, experience, and certifications — not negotiated with a recruiter.
The General Schedule (GS) is the most common federal pay scale (GS-1 to GS-15). Title 5 and some Hybrid 38 VA roles use GS grades. Pure Title 38 clinical roles use VM (Veterans Medicine) pay instead.
A must-have requirement in the JOA. Missing it = automatic disqualification, no exceptions. Common SPFs: active state license, specific degree type, clinical specialty. Always identify the SPFs before applying.
Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities — narrative essays required for many Title 5 federal jobs. Scored and used to rank candidates. Most pure Title 38 VA clinical roles are exempt. Check your JOA to confirm.
Standard Form 50 documents your federal employment history — position, grade, salary, and personnel actions. Required for current or former federal employees. Find yours through your agency's eOPF system.
The Veterans Health Administration delivers healthcare to veterans. Most clinical VA jobs are under VHA. A "VHA" prefix on an announcement number (e.g., VHA-626-24-DH-...) signals a clinical role and likely Title 38 hiring rules.
