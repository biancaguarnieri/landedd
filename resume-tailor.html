Why tailoring your resume matters more than you think

The recruiter reads your resume in 7 seconds

Research consistently puts the initial resume scan at under 10 seconds. In that window, a recruiter is looking for specific signals: does this person's background map to what I'm hiring for? Does the language match what my hiring manager told me to find?

A tailored resume puts those signals at the top and makes them impossible to miss. A generic resume makes the recruiter do the work of connecting the dots — and most won't.

What tailored actually means

Tailoring a resume doesn't mean lying or inflating your experience. It means presenting your real experience in the language of the role you're applying for. Every company, every team, and every job description has its own vocabulary — the same skill can be called stakeholder management, executive communication, or cross-functional leadership depending on the organization.

The Resume Tailor identifies those vocabulary gaps and rewrites your bullets to close them — without changing what you actually did.

How often should I tailor my resume?

Every application. This sounds like a lot of work, which is exactly why the Resume Tailor exists. What used to take 45 minutes of careful editing now takes 30 seconds. There's no longer a good reason to send a generic resume.

Keep your master resume current, then run it through the tailor for each job. Your hit rate will improve significantly.

Does it work with any job or industry?

Yes — the Resume Tailor has been tested across technology, marketing, finance, operations, healthcare, education, non-profit, and trades. The underlying logic (matching your language to the employer's language) is universal. The specific keywords and phrases differ by field, but the approach works everywhere.
